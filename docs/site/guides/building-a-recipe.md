# Building a recipe

A recipe in Brewski is made up of a few sections: basic info in the **Overview** tab, your ingredients, mash profile, and water chemistry. Add notes, track linked batches, and watch the stats sidebar update in real time as you work. Here's how it all fits together.

## Start in the Overview tab

The **Overview** tab is where you set the foundation: recipe name, style, batch size, and boil time. These numbers flow into every other calculation in the recipe, so it's worth filling them in first.

Set your batch size to your target fermenter volume. Boil time affects hop utilization and boil-off, so match it to what you actually do on brew day.

## Equipment profiles

Your equipment profile tells Brewski about your system: kettle size, trub loss, chiller loss, and boil-off rate. That information gets used to calculate volumes all the way from your water additions down to what ends up in the fermenter.

If you haven't set up a profile yet, Brewski uses sensible defaults. You'll still get useful numbers, but they'll be more accurate once you dial in your actual setup. Set one up in your settings when you're ready.

## Adding ingredients

The **Ingredients** tab holds your fermentables, hops, yeast, and misc ingredients, all in one place. Each ingredient type has its own card section on the page. Work through them in order or jump around. It doesn't matter.

Misc ingredients cover anything that doesn't fit the other categories: finings, nutrients, adjuncts, flavor additions. You can add them from the library or create your own, and specify when they get used in the process (mash, boil, fermentation, etc.).

The **Mash** tab is where you define your mash steps, temperatures, times, and water amounts.

The **Water** tab handles your water chemistry: mineral sources and ion adjustments.

## The stats sidebar

The stats sidebar updates in real time as you add and adjust ingredients. It shows:

- **OG** and **FG**: your estimated original and final gravities
- **ABV**: estimated alcohol content
- **IBU**: bitterness from hops
- **SRM**: estimated color
- **Pre-boil gravity**: useful for checking your efficiency on brew day
- **BU:GU ratio**: the balance between bitterness and gravity
- **Calories**: per serving
- **Strike temp**: the water temperature you need to hit your mash target

You don't have to hit a target for every number. Use them as a guide.

## Everything saves automatically

There's no **Save** button. Brewski saves your changes as you make them. Feel free to close a recipe and come back to it later. Everything will be right where you left it.

## Starting from a Baseline Recipe

You don't have to start from a blank page. The **Baseline Recipes** section in the sidebar includes a set of common recipes: pale ales, lagers, stouts, and more. Clone one, rename it, and build from there. It's a great way to get familiar with how a recipe comes together before you start adjusting things.

## Build it piece by piece

You don't need to fill everything in at once. Add your grain bill, see what OG looks like, then add hops and watch the IBUs climb. Build the mash profile when you're ready. Come back and add water chemistry later. Recipes are easy to adjust as you learn more about what you want.

::: tip Coming from another tool?
You can import recipes from BeerXML via **File → Import Recipe**. Most brewing apps export to BeerXML, so it's a good way to bring over recipes you already have.
:::
