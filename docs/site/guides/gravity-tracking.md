# Gravity tracking

Gravity readings are how you know what your beer is doing. They tell you when fermentation is active, when it's winding down, and when it's actually done. Here's how to use the Gravity Log tab to track it all.

## Adding a reading

Open your batch and click the **Gravity Log** tab. Hit **Add Reading**.

You'll fill in:

- **Date**: defaults to today. Change it if you're logging a reading you took earlier.
- **Gravity**: your actual measurement in whatever unit you're using.
- **Temperature** (optional): the sample temperature in degrees Celsius.
- **Notes** (optional): anything worth remembering about this reading.

Readings are listed chronologically, so you can watch the gravity drop over time.

## Gravity units

Brewski uses whichever gravity unit you've set in **Settings → Units → Gravity Unit**. You can choose between SG, Plato, or Brix. Switch the setting any time and Brewski will display everything in the new unit going forward.

## Refractometers: a caution

If you use a refractometer, know this: raw Brix readings are only accurate before fermentation starts. Once alcohol is present, it changes how light bends through the liquid, which throws off the reading.

After fermentation begins, use **Tools → Refractometer** to correct your reading before logging it. Enter your OG and your current Brix reading, and Brewski will give you the corrected gravity. Log that number, not the raw one.

If you're using a hydrometer, you don't have this problem.

## How often to take readings

A good rhythm during primary fermentation: take a reading every one to two days. You'll see the gravity drop steadily at first, then slow down as fermentation winds up.

The best way to confirm fermentation is complete: the same gravity on two or three consecutive readings. A single stable reading isn't proof. Take at least two.

Don't rush to package. A few extra days of patience is a lot cheaper than a gusher or a bottle bomb.

## Gravity and ABV

Your logged final gravity (FG) feeds into the batch's actual ABV calculation, which appears in the **Overview** tab. The more accurate your FG reading, the more accurate your ABV estimate.

::: tip Not sure if you're done?
Two identical readings taken 48 hours apart is a reliable signal. When in doubt, wait one more day.
:::
