# Scaling a recipe

Got a 5-gallon recipe you want to brew as a 10-gallon batch? Or cutting a big recipe down to a test batch? Brewski's scaling tool handles the math in one step.

## How to scale

Hit the scale icon (⤡) in the recipe header. The **Scale Recipe** modal opens with a single input: **Target Batch Size**. Type in your desired volume and hit **Scale Recipe**.

Brewski recalculates all ingredient amounts proportionally and saves the result as a new recipe version. Your original is preserved.

## What scaling affects

Everything scales linearly with the batch size:

- Fermentables
- Hops
- Yeast pitch rate
- Mash and sparge water volumes

## A few things to double-check after scaling

**Hop bitterness:** Hop amounts scale linearly, but perceived bitterness can shift at very different batch sizes. This is partly because utilization changes with wort volume and gravity. After a big scale-up or scale-down, glance at your IBU numbers and compare them to style guidelines. You may want to make small manual adjustments.

**Equipment profile:** Mash water volumes are calculated based on your equipment profile. If you're scaling to a batch size that pushes the limits of your gear, check that your profile still reflects your actual setup. A volume that works fine at 5 gallons might hit your kettle capacity at 10.

## Your original is safe

Scaling creates a new recipe version. If you want to go back to where you started, see [Recipe versions](/guides/recipe-versions).
