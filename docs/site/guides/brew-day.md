# Brew day

Brew day is where your recipe becomes beer. Brewski helps you track what's actually happening so you can compare it to what was planned. Here's how to use it effectively without making it feel like homework.

## Set the status to Brewing

Open your batch and go to the **Overview** tab. At the top you'll see the status bar showing your batch's current state. Click **Brewing** to set it.

This does two things. It tells Brewski where you are in the process, and it changes the measurements card to show the fields that actually matter right now: pre-boil gravity and post-boil volume.

## Log your pre-boil gravity

After your mash, take a gravity reading before the boil starts. Enter it in the **Pre-boil gravity** field. Brewski shows the planned value alongside it, so you can see at a glance how your efficiency is tracking.

If your pre-boil gravity is lower than planned, your mash efficiency was below target. That's worth noting so you can investigate it later. Common culprits: crush too coarse, mash temp too low, or mash time too short.

If it's higher, you're in good shape. You may end up with a slightly bigger beer than planned.

## Log your post-boil volume

After the boil, measure how much wort you've got going into the fermenter and enter it in **Post-boil volume**. This helps you track how well your system performs: boil-off rate, trub loss, and so on. Over time, your equipment profile will get dialed in.

## Switch status to Fermenting and log your OG

Once you've chilled and transferred, measure your original gravity (OG) and set the batch status to **Fermenting**. The measurements card will now show the **OG** field. Enter your reading here.

Your OG is the single most important number you'll log. It's the baseline for everything that follows: final gravity, ABV, attenuation. Even if you don't log anything else, log this.

## Use the notes field

The **Overview** tab has a notes field below the measurements card. Use it to jot down anything worth remembering: a stuck sparge, a hop addition you adjusted, equipment issues, anything that went sideways or went surprisingly well.

These notes live with the batch forever. You'll thank yourself later when you're trying to figure out why a batch tasted different or why your efficiency was off that day.

## You don't have to log everything

Brewski doesn't require you to fill in every field. If you're having a hectic brew day and all you manage to log is your OG, that's still valuable. You'll have a real efficiency record to look back on.

Log what you can. Log what matters most to you.

## After pitching, step away

Once you've pitched your yeast, you're done with brew day. Set the status to **Fermenting** if you haven't already, and close the batch. You don't need to touch it again until you're ready to start taking gravity readings.

::: tip Pre-boil gravity below target?
Try a 60-minute mash next time instead of 45, or check your mill gap if you're crushing your own grain. Small adjustments add up.
:::
