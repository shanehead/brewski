# Ingredient library

The ingredient library is Brewski's central store for every hop, fermentable, yeast, and misc ingredient available across your recipes. It ships with hundreds of entries from publicly available brewing databases, and you can add your own on top of that.

## Opening the library

Hit the flask icon in the left sidebar. The library opens with five tabs: **Hops**, **Fermentables**, **Yeast**, **Misc**, and **Water**. Use the search field to find something by name, or scroll through the list.

## Adding a custom ingredient

If you're using a hop variety or fermentable that isn't in the built-in library, add it yourself. Go to the relevant tab and hit **Add**. Fill in the name, stats, and any other fields for that ingredient type (alpha acids for hops, potential and color for fermentables, attenuation and flocculation for yeast, and so on).

Once saved, your custom ingredient shows up in the ingredient picker across all your recipes, right alongside the built-in entries.

## Editing an ingredient

Click any entry to edit it. This is handy if you want to update alpha acid values for a hop lot you just got, or correct a stat that's off.

Keep in mind: edits apply going forward. Existing recipe additions that use that ingredient aren't retroactively updated. The numbers that were in your recipe when you added the ingredient stay as they are.

## Your custom entries stay local

The built-in library comes from publicly available brewing databases and updates with the app. Your custom entries are stored in your local database and don't go anywhere. If you move your database to a cloud-synced folder, your custom ingredients move with it. See [Cloud sync](/guides/cloud-sync) for how that works.
