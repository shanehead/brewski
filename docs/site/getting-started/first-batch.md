# Your first batch

A batch is what happens when you actually brew. Where a recipe is a plan, a batch is the record of what you did: the real volumes, the real gravity numbers, and everything that happened between kettle and glass.

## Starting a batch

Open the recipe you want to brew and click **Brew** in the toolbar at the top of the page. You can also get there by opening the **Batches** tab and hitting **Brew this Recipe**. Both do the same thing.

Brewski asks how you want to version the recipe before creating the batch. If the recipe has unsaved changes, or if it's never been versioned, you'll see **Brew with current changes**. That option saves a version right now, with an optional name, then creates the batch. If you've already saved versions, **Brew a saved version** also appears so you can pick an older snapshot instead.

The batch is permanently linked to that recipe snapshot. Change the recipe later and your batch records stay untouched.

![Batches tab showing the Brew this Recipe button](/screenshots/docs-gs-batch-overview.png)

## Brew day

On brew day, open the **Overview** tab. Under the measurements section, log your pre-boil volume and gravity, then your post-boil numbers once the boil is done. These aren't required, but they're the best way to track your efficiency over time. A few batches in, you'll start to see patterns in your process.

## Fermentation and gravity readings

Once the wort is chilled and pitched, switch to the **Gravity Log** tab. Add a reading whenever you take one. Set the date, enter your gravity (Brewski accepts SG, Plato, or Brix depending on your settings), and optionally log the temperature. Plotting readings over time gives you a clear picture of fermentation progress and tells you when it's actually done rather than when you're just hoping it is.

![Gravity log with readings plotted over fermentation](/screenshots/docs-gs-batch-gravity.png)

## Packaging and carbonation

When fermentation is finished, set the batch status to **conditioning** or **packaged** in the **Overview** tab. That reveals the carbonation section at the bottom of the page. Use the priming sugar calculator to dial in your bottling additions, or use the CO2 pressure calculator if you're kegging. Enter your target volumes of CO2 and your current beer temperature, and Brewski does the rest.

![Carbonation section with priming sugar calculator](/screenshots/docs-gs-batch-carbonation.png)

## Finishing the batch

Once the beer is packaged and conditioned, set the status to **Packaged**. Add notes in the **Overview** tab while the beer is fresh in your mind. Head to the **Tasting** tab to log a rating. Over time, your batch history becomes a reference for what's working and what isn't.

## The link between batches and recipes

Your batch history is a permanent record of every beer you've actually made. Look back at any batch, even years later, and you'll see exactly what went into it.
