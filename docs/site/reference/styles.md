# Styles

Brewski ships with a full BJCP style database. Assign a style to your recipe and the stats sidebar shows you exactly where your numbers land against the official targets.

## What BJCP is

BJCP stands for Beer Judge Certification Program. They publish a widely used style guide that categorizes hundreds of beer styles with defined ranges for OG, FG, IBU, ABV, and color. Brewski uses the 2021 BJCP guidelines.

## Assigning a style to a recipe

Open a recipe and go to the **Overview** tab. Click the **Style** field to search and select a style. Once assigned, the stats sidebar updates to show the target ranges for that style alongside your recipe's calculated values.

## Reading the stats sidebar

For each stat (OG, FG, IBU, SRM), the sidebar shows:

- Your recipe's calculated value
- The style's minimum and maximum
- A visual indicator showing whether you're in range, below, or above

Green means you're in range. If you're outside the range, the indicator tells you which direction you're off. This is useful during recipe design: you can adjust hop additions, grain bill, or attenuation targets and watch the indicators update in real time.

## Styles are read-only

You can't edit BJCP style data in Brewski. The style entries use the official guidelines as published. If you want to brew to a custom target, just note it in your recipe's **Notes** field and ignore the style ranges.

## Brewing outside the guidelines

Plenty of great beer falls outside BJCP ranges. The style guidelines exist to help you compare against a known target, not to tell you what's allowed. If you're brewing a 120 IBU double IPA that breaks the style chart, that's fine. Use the notes field to document your own target ranges.
