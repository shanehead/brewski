# Unit Conversions

The **Unit Conversions** calculator handles quick conversions for volume, weight, temperature, and water:grain ratio. There's no brewing-specific logic here: it's pure unit math. You'll find it under **Tools** in the left sidebar.

## Conversions available

### Volume

| Convert | To |
|---|---|
| Liters (L) | US gallons |
| US gallons | Liters (L) |
| Milliliters (mL) | Fluid ounces (fl oz) |
| Fluid ounces (fl oz) | Milliliters (mL) |

### Weight

| Convert | To |
|---|---|
| Kilograms (kg) | Pounds (lb) |
| Pounds (lb) | Kilograms (kg) |
| Grams (g) | Ounces (oz) |
| Ounces (oz) | Grams (g) |

### Temperature

| Convert | To |
|---|---|
| Celsius (°C) | Fahrenheit (°F) |
| Fahrenheit (°F) | Celsius (°C) |

### Water:grain ratio

| Convert | To |
|---|---|
| Liters per kilogram (L/kg) | Quarts per pound (qt/lb) |
| Quarts per pound (qt/lb) | Liters per kilogram (L/kg) |

Water:grain ratio is used when calculating strike water volume for mashing. Typical mash thickness is 2.5-3.5 L/kg (roughly 1.2-1.7 qt/lb).

## Tips

**Brewski uses metric throughout.** All recipe inputs and calculator outputs are in metric units. Use this calculator if you're working from an imperial recipe or a spec sheet in US units and need to convert your inputs before entering them.

**Temperature matters for strike water.** If you're calculating mash temperature manually or adjusting a recipe from an imperial source, use this calculator to convert your target strike temperature before plugging it into your strike water calculation.
